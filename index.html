Introduction

This Online Appendix accompanies Some Respect Reserved: Survey Experiment Insights on the Perception of Creative Commons and Copyright Infringement, forthcoming in the Cardozo Arts & Entertainment Law Journal. It also documents the analysis in the dissertation chapter Hidden Costs of Open Licensing? A Survey Experiment on Creative Commons and Copyright Infringement in Creative Realism: Innovation Beyond the Ownership Incentive. The Author conveys his gratitude to the Journal’s editors and the dissertation committee members (Aaron Shaw, Aymar Jean Christian, and Matthew Kugler) and other scholars who have provided feedback on the Article and the underlying research. The Author has also benefited from presentations and discussion of this research with:

  • Northwestern Pritzker School of Law’s J.D./Ph.D. Seminar, as well attendees of internal job talk rehearsals, and the School of Communication’s Community Data Science Collective
  • The faculties of the University of Oregon’s School of Journalism and Communication, the Ohio State University Moritz School of Law, and the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign
  • The Law and Society Association’s 2025 Annual Meeting and the Intellectual Property Scholars Conference 2025.

All errors and omissions are the Author’s alone.

The Appendix is organized as a reproducible Quarto Book. Every figure and table in the Article has a corresponding section here where the underlying data, code, and full statistical output are presented. Statistical results are generated directly from the underlying data. The code used to generate those results can be viewed in the source code of each chapter. The Appendix also includes detailed documentation of the survey instrument, data collection, and sample characteristics. The dataset and survey instruments are freely licensed for adaptation or replication.

The pre-registration for the survey experiment is available at the Open Science Foundation: https://osf.io/snxyt/. Some variations from the pre-registration are documented in the relevant sections of this Appendix, including the substitution of Group B1 for Group B in the Educational Use scenario analysis due to an error in data collection.

Some Respect Reserved

Thomas H. Rousse, Some Respect Reserved: Survey Experiment Insights on the Perception of Creative Commons and Copyright Infringement. 44 Cardozo Arts & Entertainment Law Journal __ (forthcoming 2026).

The Article reports results from a pre-registered survey experiment (n = 1,239) examining the American public’s recognition of the Creative Commons brand and logo before being randomly split into treatment groups. Respondents were assigned to one of three treatment groups — CC Short (Group A), CC Full Text + Primer (Group B), and Copyright Marking Control (Group C) — and evaluated seven licensing scenarios. The study tests whether exposure to CC license terms affects perceptions of legal risk, preferred outcomes, and price estimates.
