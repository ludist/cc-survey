8  Question Summaries

This Chapter consolidates the per-scenario results from Chapters 7.1–7.7 into cross-scenario summary tables for each question type. The tables replicate the Article’s summary tables programmatically from the underlying data.

Format conventions:

  • Q1–Q3 summary tables: One row per scenario and group. Mean, SD, and Median in separate columns. Treatment groups show [% difference from control] after the mean with significance stars from Dunn’s post-hoc tests (Holm-adjusted).
  • Delta summary table: Same format. Brackets show the mean delta as a percentage of the group’s Q2 mean — the fraction of perceived consequences that exceeds respondent preferences.
  • Q4 summary table: One row per scenario and group. Median and SD after 99th-percentile outlier trimming. Treatment groups show [% difference from control median] with significance stars.
  • VDA tables: One row per scenario and pairwise comparison. P(1st > 2nd) is the probability that a randomly selected observation from the first-named group exceeds one from the second-named group (Vargha & Delaney’s A, expressed as a percentage). 50% indicates no difference. Effect size thresholds follow Vargha & Delaney (2000): small \(\geq\) .56, moderate \(\geq\) .64, large \(\geq\) .71.

Significance notation: * p < .05, ** p < .01, *** p < .001; n.s. = not significant. Stars appear after [% difference] in summary tables and in the Sig. column of VDA and Wilcoxon tables.

NoteEducational Use Scenario

Rows marked with a dagger (†) use supplemental Group B1 (n = 97) in place of Group B. See §3.1 for the B1 substitution.
